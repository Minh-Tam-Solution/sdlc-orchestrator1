## Universal Role-Based Execution with Coordinated CPO/CTO Governance

**SDLC Version**: 4.3  
**Date**: September 13, 2025  
**Status**: Completed Upgrade (Production-Ready)  
**Scope**: BFlow 2.0 platform, full lifecycle governance and compliance  
**Owners**: CPO Office (Governance), CTO Office (Technical Execution)

---

## 🎯 Executive Summary

The BFlow 2.0 platform successfully upgraded from SDLC 4.2 (Design-First Enhanced Framework) to SDLC 4.3 (Universal Role-Based Execution Framework). This upgrade formalized personnel-agnostic role execution, clarified CPO/CTO joint governance, and operationalized universal compliance monitoring and executive visibility. The transition eliminated version drift, standardized role templates, and strengthened enforcement of quality gates across teams and modules.

---

## 🔎 Why Upgrade from 4.2 → 4.3

- 4.2 achievements: Strong design-first discipline, specialized role templates, quality gates.  
- 4.3 goals: Universal role-based execution (human/AI interchangeable), executive command center, scalable governance, and portfolio-level visibility.  
- Business driver: Enterprise-scale coordination with predictable quality and compliance across multiple parallel initiatives.

---

## 🤝 CPO/CTO Coordination Model (4.3)

- CPO: Policy owner for SDLC governance; defines quality gates and compliance thresholds; enforces NO-DOC/NO-DESIGN = NO-MERGE; approves framework changes.  
- CTO: Technology owner for execution; ensures architecture, performance, security; implements role tooling and measurement; operates executive dashboards.  
- Joint mechanisms: Universal compliance scanner, quality gate enforcer, portfolio dashboards, weekly governance review, escalation matrix, rollback plans.

---

## 🧭 Upgrade Scope and Artifacts

- Templates upgraded to 4.3: Cursor CPO, GitHub Copilot CTO, Claude role templates (Technical Writer, Software Architect, Developer, DevOps, QA, Conductor CPO/CTO).  
- Summary and guides aligned to 4.3: Framework summary, implementation guide, training & deployment materials.  
- Documentation standards: Introduced `FRAMEWORK-CONTROLS-4.3.md` with universal role-based enforcement language.  
- Continuous Improvement: Updated upgrade guide and CI processes to monitor contract and documentation drift vs 4.3.

---

## 🛠️ Implementation Highlights

1) Version normalization  
- Replaced residual “4.2” labels inside 4.3-named documents.  
- Ensured all role templates reference SDLC 4.3 Universal Role-Based Execution Framework.

2) Governance activation  
- Enabled portfolio-level compliance scanning and reporting.  
- Documented executive escalation triggers and rollback procedures.

3) Contract and documentation integrity  
- Enforced pre-commit documentation checks for router/schema changes.  
- Added nightly drift scan between runtime contracts and versioned specs.

4) Performance and reliability objectives  
- Maintained <50ms API target, 99.9% availability standards in 4.3 docs.  
- Captured p95 targets and error budgets per module in design docs.

---

## 📈 Outcomes

- Universal applicability: Role execution templates now personnel-agnostic (human/AI).  
- Executive visibility: CTO/CPO dashboards and alerts for compliance, coordination, and quality.  
- Quality consistency: Cross-team quality gates with automated enforcement.  
- Reduced drift: Documentation/spec alignment checks at commit, nightly, and weekly cadences.
