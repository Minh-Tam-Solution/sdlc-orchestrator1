## 📊 Empathy Map Generation

### Standard Empathy Map Prompt

```
Create comprehensive empathy map from research:

User Segment: [describe target users]

Research Summary:
[paste interview transcripts, survey responses, observation notes]

Generate Empathy Map with 4 quadrants:

1. THINK (Thoughts & Beliefs)
   - What occupies user's thoughts
   - Beliefs about the problem
   - Assumptions they hold
   - Concerns and worries

2. FEEL (Emotions)
   - Emotional state when facing problem
   - Frustrations and anxieties
   - Hopes and aspirations
   - Fear factors

3. SAY (Verbal Expression)
   - Direct quotes from research
   - Common phrases they use
   - How they describe the problem
   - Words that matter to them

4. DO (Actions & Behaviors)
   - Current behaviors
   - Workarounds they've created
   - Tools they use
   - Processes they follow

Also include:
- Pains (obstacles, risks, frustrations)
- Gains (wants, needs, measures of success)

Evidence: Link each insight to specific research data
```

## 🔍 Research Synthesis Examples

### Example 1: SME Software Adoption

```
Research Context:
- 15 interviews with Vietnamese SME owners
- Industry: Manufacturing & retail
- Problem space: Business software adoption

Synthesis Prompt:
"Synthesize research from 15 Vietnamese SME owners:

Key Quotes:
- "Software is too complicated, takes 3 months to learn"
- "My staff is 45-60 years old, not computer-savvy"
- "We tried 3 different systems, all failed"
- "I need results in 1 week, not 3 months"

Behaviors Observed:
- Still using paper + Excel for everything
- Hiring external consultants monthly
- Training budget: $0 (can't afford)
- Decision maker: owner (50-65 years old)

Pain Points:
- Software too complex → 3 months training needed
- Staff resistance → 60% won't adopt new tools
- No Vietnamese support → can't get help
- Cost uncertain → afraid of hidden fees

Generate empathy map, persona, and top 3 insights
Focus on cultural factors (Vietnamese family business context)"
```

### Example 2: Developer Tool Usage

```
Research Context:
- 20 interviews with software developers
- Focus: Development workflow pain points
- Mix of solo, startup, enterprise developers

Synthesis Prompt:
"Analyze developer workflow research:

Developer Segments:
- Solo: 1 dev + AI tools
- Startup: 2-5 devs, fast iteration
- Enterprise: 15+ devs, compliance focus

Common Quotes:
- "Spend 40% time on boilerplate code"
- "Can't find good Vietnamese AI coding assistant"
- "Code review takes 2 days, blocks everything"
- "Each framework has different patterns"

Tool Stack Observed:
- IDE: VSCode (95%), Cursor (growing)
- AI: ChatGPT (85%), Claude (45%), Copilot (30%)
- Design: Figma (70%), no automated conversion
- Testing: Manual (60%), some automation (40%)

Pain Points:
- Repetitive coding → 10-20 hours/week wasted
- Design-to-code manual → 2-4 hours per component
- Code review slow → 2-day average wait
- Tool fragmentation → 8-10 different tools

Generate insights focusing on productivity gains
Target: 10x-50x improvement potential"
```

## ✅ Quality Checklist

Ensure your empathy synthesis includes:

### Data Quality
- ✅ Multiple data sources (interviews, observations, surveys)
- ✅ Sufficient sample size (10+ users minimum)
- ✅ Direct quotes included (verbatim)
- ✅ Behavioral data, not just opinions
- ✅ Context provided (when, where, why)

### Insight Quality
- ✅ Specific, not generic
- ✅ Evidence-based, not assumptions
- ✅ Actionable, points to solutions
- ✅ User-centered, not technology-first
- ✅ Surprising or non-obvious

### Persona Quality
- ✅ Based on real research, not stereotypes
- ✅ Detailed enough to guide decisions
- ✅ Representative of target segment
- ✅ Includes goals AND frustrations
- ✅ Memorable and relatable

### Empathy Map Quality
- ✅ All 4 quadrants complete
- ✅ Specific details, not generalities
- ✅ Internally consistent
- ✅ Reveals contradictions where they exist
- ✅ Links to research evidence
