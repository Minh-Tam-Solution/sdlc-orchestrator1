# 🎭 User Testing Script Template

**Purpose**: Standardized script for conducting user testing sessions
**When to Use**: TEST phase (SDLC Stage 03 - BUILD)
**Time Required**: 45-60 minutes per session
**Participants**: Facilitator + Note-Taker + 1 User

---

## 📋 How to Use This Script

1. **Customize for your prototype** - Replace [brackets] with your specifics
2. **Practice before first session** - Facilitator should rehearse 2-3 times
3. **Stay flexible** - Script is guide, not rigid rules (adapt to user behavior)
4. **Record everything** - Video + screen recording + notes (with consent)
5. **Debrief after each session** - Note-taker and facilitator align on findings

---

## 🎬 SESSION SCRIPT

### PART 1: INTRODUCTION (5 minutes)

**Goal**: Build rapport, set expectations, obtain consent

---

#### 1A: Welcome & Thank You (1 min)

**Facilitator says**:
```
"Hi [Participant Name], welcome! Thank you so much for joining us today.
My name is [Your Name], and this is [Note-Taker Name] who will be taking
notes during our session.

We really appreciate you taking the time to help us test this new app.
Your feedback is incredibly valuable to us."
```

**Body language**: Smile, make eye contact, warm tone

---

#### 1B: Explain Purpose (2 min)

**Facilitator says**:
```
"Today, we're going to show you a prototype of a new attendance check-in
app for restaurant staff. This is an early version - not the final product -
and we're testing it to understand what works and what doesn't.

I want to emphasize: WE ARE TESTING THE APP, NOT YOU. There are no right
or wrong answers. If something is confusing, that's the app's fault, not yours.
In fact, if you get stuck, that's exactly what we need to know!

Our goal today is to see how you would use this app in your daily work,
and to get your honest feedback. Does that make sense?"
```

**Wait for participant to nod/confirm understanding**

---

#### 1C: Obtain Consent (2 min)

**Facilitator says**:
```
"Before we start, I need to go over a quick consent form. We'd like to
record this session (video and screen) so we can review your feedback later.
The recording is ONLY for our team - we won't share it publicly or use it
for marketing.

Also, your responses will be kept confidential. We'll use a participant ID
(not your real name) when we share findings with our team.

Are you comfortable with us recording this session?"
```

**If YES**:
- Hand consent form to participant
- "Please sign here indicating you agree"
- Provide copy to participant, keep one

**If NO**:
- "No problem! We can proceed without recording. We'll just take notes."
- Ask: "Are you comfortable with us taking written notes?"

**Start recording** (if consent obtained)

**Facilitator says**:
```
"Great! I'm starting the recording now. For the record, today is [Date],
and we're conducting a user test of [Product Name] with Participant [ID].
Participant has provided consent to record this session."
```

---

### PART 2: CONTEXT QUESTIONS (5 minutes)

**Goal**: Understand current process, build empathy, establish baseline

---

#### 2A: Current Process

**Facilitator says**:
```
"Before we look at the prototype, I'd like to understand how you currently
do things. Can you walk me through how you check in for your shift today?"
```

**Probing questions** (if needed):
- "What time do you usually arrive?"
- "Where do you go first when you arrive?"
- "What do you have to do before starting work?"
- "How do you record that you've arrived?"

**Note-taker captures**: Current process steps, pain points mentioned, emotions

---

#### 2B: Pain Points

**Facilitator says**:
```
"What's the most frustrating part of the current check-in process?"
```

**Probing questions**:
- "How often does that happen?"
- "What do you do when [pain point] occurs?"
- "Have you found any workarounds?"

**Note-taker captures**: Specific pain points, frequency, workarounds

---

#### 2C: Trust & Accuracy

**Facilitator says**:
```
"Do you feel confident that your hours are recorded accurately?
Why or why not?"
```

**Probing questions**:
- "Have you ever had a dispute about your hours?"
- "What happened?"
- "How was it resolved?"

**Note-taker captures**: Trust level, past disputes, emotional reactions

---

### PART 3: PROTOTYPE DEMONSTRATION (5 minutes)

**Goal**: Show how prototype works, set context for tasks

---

#### 3A: Introduction to Prototype

**Facilitator says**:
```
"Now I'm going to show you the prototype we've built. Remember, this is
an early version - some things might not work perfectly, and that's okay.
We want your honest reactions.

This app is designed to [one-sentence value proposition].

Let me show you how it works."
```

**Example**:
```
"This app is designed to let you check in for your shift using your
smartphone, with automatic GPS and photo verification, so you don't
have to fill out paper forms."
```

---

#### 3B: Quick Walkthrough

**Facilitator demonstrates ONE complete task**:

**Facilitator says** (while demonstrating):
```
"Here's the home screen. When you arrive for your shift, you tap this
'Check In' button. [TAP]

The app uses your phone's camera to take a quick photo, and it automatically
records your location using GPS. [SHOW]

Then you confirm by tapping here. [TAP]

And you're checked in! You can see your check-in time and location here. [POINT]

Okay, now it's your turn to try."
```

**Hand device to participant**

---

### PART 4: USER TASKS (25 minutes)

**Goal**: Observe participant using prototype, identify usability issues

---

#### TASK STRUCTURE (for each task):

1. **Read task** (don't explain how to do it)
2. **Let participant attempt** (hands-off, don't help)
3. **Think Aloud prompt** (if participant is silent)
4. **Observe & note** (completion, time, errors, emotion)
5. **Probe** (after task, ask follow-up questions)

---

#### 4A: Task 1 - Primary Use Case

**Facilitator says**:
```
"Imagine you just arrived for your morning shift. Using this app, show me
how you would check in."
```

**DO NOT** say: "Tap the Check In button" or give any hints

**If participant is silent**, prompt:
```
"Can you tell me what you're thinking right now?"
```

**If participant gets stuck (>30 seconds of confusion)**:
```
"What are you looking for?"
"What would you expect to happen next?"
```

**Only provide hint after 60+ seconds of struggle**:
```
"For this prototype, you'd tap [X]. But it's helpful that you expected [Y] -
we'll consider that in our design."
```

**After task completion** (success or failure):

**Facilitator asks**:
```
- "How was that experience? Easy or difficult?"
- "On a scale of 1-5 (1=very difficult, 5=very easy), how would you rate that?"
- "Was anything confusing or surprising?"
- "Did that feel like what you expected?"
```

**Note-taker records**:
- ⏱️ **Time**: [X seconds]
- ✅/❌ **Completion**: [Success without help / Success with hint / Failure]
- 😊/😐/😞 **Emotion**: [Confident / Neutral / Frustrated]
- 💬 **Quote**: ["This is easy!" or "I don't understand..."]
- 🐛 **Issues**: [Specific usability problems observed]

---

#### 4B: Task 2 - Secondary Use Case

**Facilitator says**:
```
"Great! Now imagine it's the end of your shift. You want to see how many
hours you worked today. How would you do that?"
```

**Repeat same observation process**:
- Hands-off, let them explore
- Think Aloud prompts
- Observe time, completion, emotion
- Ask follow-up questions

**After task**:
```
- "Did you find what you were looking for?"
- "Is this information useful to you?"
- "What would you want to do with this information?"
```

---

#### 4C: Task 3 - Error Recovery

**Facilitator introduces problem**:
```
"Imagine you tapped Check In, but the app said 'GPS not available.'
What would you do?"
```

**Or trigger an error in the prototype** (if possible):
- Disable GPS
- Simulate "Location not found"
- Observe how user reacts

**After observing**:
```
- "What did you expect to happen?"
- "How would you fix this?"
- "Would you try again, or give up and use the old paper process?"
```

**This reveals**:
- Error message clarity
- User mental model
- Tolerance for friction

---

#### 4D: Task 4 - Exploration

**Facilitator says**:
```
"You have 2 minutes to explore the app freely. Poke around, try different
screens. Let me know if you discover anything interesting or confusing."
```

**Step back, observe silently**

**After 2 minutes**:
```
- "What did you notice?"
- "Was there anything you expected to see but didn't find?"
- "Any features that surprised you?"
```

**This reveals**:
- Undiscovered features
- Navigation issues
- Unmet expectations

---

### PART 5: FEEDBACK INTERVIEW (15 minutes)

**Goal**: Gather qualitative insights, understand emotional reactions

---

#### 5A: Overall Impression

**Facilitator says**:
```
"Now that you've used the app, I'd love to hear your overall thoughts.

First, in one word, how would you describe this app?"
```

**Wait for response** (e.g., "Easy", "Confusing", "Helpful", "Invasive")

**Follow-up**:
```
"Can you tell me more about why you chose that word?"
```

---

#### 5B: Comparison to Current Process

**Facilitator says**:
```
"Compared to your current check-in process (the paper forms), would you
prefer to use this app? Why or why not?"
```

**Probing questions**:
- "What's better about the app?"
- "What's better about the current process?"
- "What would make you switch to using the app?"

---

#### 5C: Trust & Transparency (Critical for NQH-Bot)

**Facilitator says**:
```
"The app takes a photo and records your GPS location when you check in.
Does that feel like TRANSPARENCY (helping verify your hours) or
SURVEILLANCE (big brother watching)?"
```

**Wait for answer**

**Follow-up based on response**:

**If "Surveillance"**:
```
"What about it feels like surveillance?"
"What would make it feel less invasive?"
```

**If "Transparency"**:
```
"What makes it feel transparent?"
"Would you want your manager to be able to see your photo/location?"
```

**This is CRITICAL assumption to validate**

---

#### 5D: Willingness to Use Daily

**Facilitator says**:
```
"Imagine this app was available tomorrow. Would you use it every day?
Be honest - we won't be offended!"
```

**If YES**:
```
"What would make you excited to use it?"
"Would you recommend it to your colleagues?"
```

**If NO**:
```
"What would prevent you from using it?"
"What would need to change for you to use it?"
```

**If MAYBE**:
```
"What would push you from 'maybe' to 'yes'?"
```

---

#### 5E: Feature Requests

**Facilitator says**:
```
"If you could change ONE thing about this app, what would it be?"
```

**Wait for response**

**Then**:
```
"If you could add ONE feature that's not currently there, what would it be?"
```

**Note-taker captures**: Requested changes and additions

---

#### 5F: Concerns & Blockers

**Facilitator says**:
```
"What worries you about using this app? Anything that makes you hesitant?"
```

**Probing questions**:
- "Do you have concerns about privacy?"
- "What if your phone battery dies?"
- "What if you forget your phone at home?"

**This reveals blockers that could kill adoption**

---

### PART 6: WRAP-UP (5 minutes)

**Goal**: Thank participant, explain next steps, provide incentive

---

#### 6A: Final Thoughts

**Facilitator says**:
```
"Is there anything else you'd like to share that we haven't talked about?
Any last thoughts or questions?"
```

---

#### 6B: Thank You & Explain Next Steps

**Facilitator says**:
```
"Thank you so much for your time and honest feedback today. You've given
us incredibly valuable insights that will help us improve the app.

We're going to take your feedback and the feedback from [X other participants],
synthesize all the learnings, and make improvements. In about 2-3 weeks,
we'd love to show you the updated version. Would you be willing to do a
quick 15-minute follow-up session?"
```

**If YES**: Get contact info, note in participant file

---

#### 6C: Provide Incentive

**Facilitator says**:
```
"As a thank you for your time, here's a [₫200,000 Grab gift card / cash / etc.].
We really appreciate your help!"
```

**Hand over incentive**

---

#### 6D: Stop Recording

**Facilitator says**:
```
"I'm going to stop the recording now. [STOP RECORDING]

Thank you again! Feel free to reach out if you have any questions later.
Here's my contact info."
```

**Escort participant out / end video call**

## 📊 POST-SESSION DEBRIEF

**Immediately after participant leaves (5-10 minutes):**

### Debrief Template:

**Facilitator & Note-Taker discuss**:

```yaml
Participant ID: [P04]
Date: [Nov 20, 2025]

Top 3 Observations:
  1. [Observation 1 - what stood out?]
  2. [Observation 2]
  3. [Observation 3]

Critical Issues (Blockers):
  - [Issue 1 - would prevent daily use]
  - [Issue 2]

Positive Surprises:
  - [What worked better than expected?]

Key Quotes:
  - "[Direct quote 1]"
  - "[Direct quote 2]"

Changes to Script for Next Session:
  - [Anything to adjust in script or tasks?]
```

**Example**:
```yaml
Participant ID: P04 (Restaurant Staff - Location B)
Date: Nov 20, 2025

Top 3 Observations:
  1. Completed check-in in 8 seconds (under 10-second target!) ✅
  2. Said "This feels like proof I was here" - transparency, not surveillance ✅
  3. Struggled to find Hours History (took 45 seconds, expected tab navigation)

Critical Issues:
  - Concerned about phone battery: "What if my phone dies during shift?" 🚨
  - Wants ability to check in WITHOUT photo (sometimes looks messy) 🚨

Positive Surprises:
  - Loved GPS verification - "Manager can't say I wasn't here"
  - Appreciated seeing exact check-in time immediately

Key Quotes:
  - "This is way easier than paper forms!"
  - "I trust this more than my manager's memory"
  - "Can I download this app today?" (wanted to use immediately)

Changes for Next Session:
  - Ask earlier about battery concerns (seems to be pattern)
  - Add task: "Check in without taking photo" (if feature exists)
```

## ✅ TESTING BEST PRACTICES

### DO's ✅:

- ✅ **Stay neutral**: Don't lead participant, don't defend design
- ✅ **Embrace silence**: Let participant think, don't rush to fill gaps
- ✅ **Observe body language**: Frustration, confusion, delight show before words
- ✅ **Probe deeper**: Ask "Why?" at least 5 times
- ✅ **Take breaks**: If 3+ sessions in a day, take 15-min breaks between

### DON'Ts ❌:

- ❌ **Don't explain how to do tasks**: "Tap here" defeats the purpose
- ❌ **Don't defend design**: "But we designed it this way because..." - listen, don't justify
- ❌ **Don't lead with solutions**: "Would a button work better?" - let them suggest
- ❌ **Don't skip recording**: Memory is unreliable, capture everything
- ❌ **Don't test when tired**: Facilitator fatigue leads to missed insights

## 💡 PRO TIPS

### Tip 1: "Think Aloud" is Gold
> "If participant is silent, gently prompt: 'What are you thinking?' Their inner monologue reveals mental model."

### Tip 2: Watch Hands, Not Face
> "Hesitations, repeated taps, scrolling back-and-forth reveal confusion better than words."

### Tip 3: Test in Real Context
> "Test attendance app during actual shift change at restaurant. Real context reveals real issues."

### Tip 4: The 5-Second Rule
> "If participant doesn't act within 5 seconds of task prompt, they're confused. Note it."

### Tip 5: Record Everything, Review Selectively
> "Record all sessions, but only re-watch confusing moments. Saves time, preserves accuracy."
