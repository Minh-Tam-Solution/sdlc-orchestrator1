## ⚠️ CPO Concerns & Risk Management

### What I'm Watching Closely

**1. Change Management Risk** (My biggest concern)

```yaml
Risk: Team resistance to new tool
  - "Another AI tool to learn"
  - "Why not stick with what works"
  - "Trust issues with AI reviews"

Mitigation Strategy:
  1. Voluntary pilot (no force)
  2. Transparent communication
  3. Show value quickly
  4. Address concerns openly
  5. Make it optional initially

CPO Commitment:
  - If team hates it, we stop
  - Quality of work-life matters
  - Trust team's judgment
  - Balance efficiency with satisfaction
```

**2. False Positive Frustration**

```yaml
Risk: Developers annoyed by wrong suggestions
  - Wastes time
  - Erodes trust
  - Reduces adoption

Mitigation:
  - Aggressive tuning in Week 1
  - Easy dismissal mechanism
  - Track dismissal patterns
  - Disable bad rules quickly

CPO Metric:
  - If >5 false positives/PR → Too high
  - Need <3 to be net positive
  - Will monitor closely
```

**3. ROI Validation**

```yaml
Risk: Benefits don't materialize
  - Time savings overstated
  - Quality gains minimal
  - Costs underestimated

Mitigation:
  - Measure actual time (not estimates)
  - Track bugs caught
  - Document all costs
  - Conservative analysis

CPO Decision:
  - If <20% time savings → Not worth it
  - Need ≥30% to justify
  - Will make data-driven call
```

**4. Strategic Distraction**

```yaml

  - 12 hours CTO time

  - Opportunity cost?

Perspective:
  - 24 hours investment
  - Potential 2,700 hours/year savings
```
